Chikonohono Main Church

Welcome to Chikonohono Main Church, a warm and welcoming community of faith. We invite you to explore our website and learn more about who we are, what we believe, and how you can connect with us.

Our Purpose.

Inspired by the everlasting gospel message (Revelation 14:6), Chikonohono Adventist Church serves as a beacon of hope in Chinhoyi. We actively share God's love and grace through acts of service, nurturing fellowship, and teaching the Bible's message, striving to meet the needs of our community.

Our Mission.

Following Jesus' Commission (Matthew 28:18-19), Chikonohono Adventist Church welcomes you to join our Christ-centered community in Chinhoyi. We believe in sharing the good news of Jesus Christ, celebrating new beginnings through baptism, and growing together in faith through worship services, Bible studies, and fellowship. We strive to live by Christ's example, extending a helping hand to our neighbors and actively serving the Chinhoyi community.

And Jesus came and spake unto them, saying, All power is given unto me in heaven and in earth. Therefore go ye, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost:

Matthew 28:18-19 KJV

What we believe.

The Doctrine of God:

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son (Jesus Christ), and Holy Spirit (Exodus 20:3, 1 John 5:7). This triune God is our Creator, Redeemer, and Sustainer. He is all-powerful, all-knowing, and ever-present, characterized by perfect love, justice, and mercy.

The Doctrine of Man:

We believe God created humans in His image, perfect and sinless (Genesis 1:27). However, through free will, humanity chose to disobey God, introducing sin and its consequences into the world (Romans 5:12). Despite this fall, God desires to restore humanity to fellowship with Him.

The Doctrine of Salvation:

We believe salvation is God's free gift offered to all through faith in Jesus Christ (Ephesians 2:8-9). His sacrificial death on the cross provides forgiveness of sins and reconciliation with God (Romans 5:1-11). Through repentance and acceptance of His grace, we are transformed and empowered to live a new life for Him.

The Doctrine of the Church:

We believe the church is the community of believers united by faith in Jesus Christ (Ephesians 1:22-23). This community is called to worship God, proclaim the gospel message, nurture believers, and serve others (1 Corinthians 12:27-31). Chikonohono Adventist Church strives to be a vibrant and welcoming expression of this global Christian community.

The Doctrine of Christian Living:

We believe God desires our lives to reflect His character. The Holy Spirit empowers us to live a life guided by God's law (the Ten Commandments) and the teachings of Jesus (Matthew 22:37-40). This includes acts of love, service, and compassion towards others (Galatians 5:22-23).

The Doctrine of Last Day Events:

We believe in the literal, visible return of Jesus Christ to Earth (1 Thessalonians 4:13-18). This second coming signifies the end of evil and the resurrection of the dead (John 5:28-29). The righteous will inherit eternal life in God's new kingdom, while the unrepentant will face final judgment (Revelation 20:11-15).


Our values.

Open and Accepting:.

We believe everyone deserves a warm welcome, regardless of background or beliefs. We strive to foster a safe and supportive environment where everyone feels valued and respected.

Christ-Centered:

Jesus Christ is the foundation of our faith and the core of our teachings. We are dedicated to studying the Bible, following Christ's teachings, and sharing the message of hope and love with our community.

Service-Oriented:

We believe in demonstrating compassion through acts of service. We encourage our members to use their talents and resources to help others within the church and the broader Chinhoyi community.

Growing Together:

We believe in the importance of spiritual growth and lifelong learning. We offer opportunities for fellowship, worship, and Bible study to nurture individual and collective faith journeys.
